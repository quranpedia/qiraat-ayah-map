## English translation

Sūrat al-Ḥamd.

Medinan: this is the view of Abū Hurayra, Mujāhid, and ʿAṭāʾ b. Yasār. Ibn ʿAbbās and Qatāda said that it is Makkan. Its counterpart (naẓīrah) in the number of its verses, in the Makki and Shami counts, is Sūrat al-Nās; in the Kufi and Basri counts, Sūrat Arāʾayta; and it has no counterpart in the two Madani counts. Its words are twenty-five, equal to the word-count of Arāʾayta, and its letters are one hundred and twenty. It is seven verses in all systems of counting.

Its points of disagreement are two verses: “Bism Allāh al-Raḥmān al-Raḥīm” is counted by the Makki and Kufi and not counted by the others; “Anʿamta ʿalayhim” is not counted by the Makki and Kufi, but the others count it.

In it there is one place that resembles a verse-ending without being counted, by consensus: namely His saying, “Iyyāka naʿbudu.”
