## English translation

Sūrat al-Anfāl.

Medinan. Its counterpart (naẓīrah) in the two Madani counts is Sūrat al-Ḥajj; in the Kufi count, Sūrat al-Zumar; and in the Shami count, Sūrat al-Furqān. It has no counterpart in the Makki and Basri counts.

Its words are one thousand two hundred and thirty-one, and its letters are five thousand two hundred and ninety-four. It is seventy-five verses in the Kufi count; seventy-six in the two Madani counts, the Makki, and the Basri; and seventy-seven in the Shami count.

Its points of disagreement are three verse-endings: “thumma yughlabūn” is counted by the Basri and the Shami and not counted by the others; “li-yaqḍiya llāhu amran kāna mafʿūlan” is not counted by the Kufi and is counted by the others; and “bi-naṣrihi wa-bi-l-muʾminīn” is not counted by the Basri and is counted by the others.

In it there are, by consensus, eight places that resemble verse-endings without being counted: “ulāʾika humu l-muʾminūn”; “rijza l-shayṭān”; “fawqa l-aʿnāq”; “ʿani l-masjidi l-ḥarām”; “illā l-muttaqūn”; “yawma l-furqān”; “yawma l-taqā l-jamʿān”; and the second occurrence of “amran kāna mafʿūlan,” namely the one after which comes “wa-ilā llāhi turjaʿu l-umūr.”
