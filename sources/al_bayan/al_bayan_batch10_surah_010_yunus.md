## English translation

Sūrat Yūnus.

Makkan. Its counterpart (naẓīrah) in the Shami count alone is Sūrat Subḥān, and it has no counterpart in any other count. Its words are one thousand eight hundred and thirty-two, and its letters are seven thousand five hundred and sixty-seven, the same as the letters of Hūd. It is one hundred and ten verses in the Shami count and one hundred and nine in the count of the others.

Its points of disagreement are three verse-endings: “mukhliṣīna lahu l-dīn” is counted by the Shami and not counted by the others; “la-nakūnanna mina l-shākirīn” is not counted by the Shami but counted by the others; and “wa-shifāʾun limā fī l-ṣudūr” is counted by the Shami and not counted by the others.

In it there is, by consensus, one place that resembles a verse-ending without being counted, namely His saying “wa-la-qad bawwaʾnā banī Isrāʾīl.”

All of the counting systems do not count “alif lām rāʾ” and “alif lām mīm rāʾ” as independent verses in the six surahs where those openings occur.
