## English translation

Sūrat al-Baqara.

Medinan. It has no counterpart (naẓīrah) in the number of its verses. Its words are six thousand one hundred and twenty-one, and its letters are twenty-five thousand five hundred. It is two hundred and eighty-five verses in the two Madani counts, the Makki count, and the Shami count; two hundred and eighty-six in the Kufi count; and two hundred and eighty-seven in the Basri count.

Its points of disagreement are eleven verse-endings: “Alif Lām Mīm” is counted by the Kufi and not counted by the others; “ʿAdhābun alīm” is counted by the Shami and not counted by the others; “Muṣliḥūn” is not counted by the Shami, while the others count it; “Illā khāʾifīn” is counted by the Basri and not counted by the others; “Yā ulī al-albāb” is not counted by the first Madani and the Makki, while the others count it; the second occurrence of “Min khalāq” is not counted by the later Madani, while the others count it; the second occurrence of “Mādhā yunfiqūn” is counted by the first Madani and the Makki and not counted by the others; the first occurrence of “Laʿallakum tatafakkarūn” is counted by the later Madani, the Kufi, and the Shami and not counted by the others; “Qawlan maʿrūfan” is counted by the Basri and not counted by the others; “Al-ḥayyu al-qayyūm” is counted by the later Madani, the Makki, and the Basri and not counted by the others—and they unanimously count it in Āl ʿImrān and unanimously omit it in Ṭā Hā; “Min al-ẓulumāti ilā al-nūr” is counted by the first Madani and not counted by the others.

In it there are twelve places that resemble verse-endings without being counted, by consensus: the first occurrence of “Mā lahu fī al-ākhirati min khalāq”; “Wa-hum yatlūna al-kitāb”; “Fa-innamā hum fī shiqāq”; “Wa-al-anfusi wa-al-thamarāt”; “Fī buṭūnihim illā al-nār”; “Ṭaʿāmu miskīn”; “Min al-hudā wa-al-furqān”; “Wa-al-ḥurumātu qiṣāṣ”; “ʿInda al-mashʿari al-ḥarām”; the third occurrence of “Al-khabītha minhu tunfiqūn”; the first occurrence of “Wa-yasʾalūnaka mādhā yunfiqūn”; and “Wa-lā shahīd.” It is also said that the Makki count “Wa-lā shahīd,” but that is not sound.
