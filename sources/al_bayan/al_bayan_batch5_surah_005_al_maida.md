## English translation

Sūrat al-Māʾida.

Medinan, except for one verse from it that was sent down at ʿArafa: namely His saying, exalted is He, “al-yawma akmaltu lakum dīnakum” up to His saying, “wa-raḍītu lakumu l-islāma dīnan.” ʿAbd al-Raḥmān b. Khālid related to us; he said: Aḥmad b. Jaʿfar related to us; he said: ʿAbd Allāh b. Aḥmad related to us; he said: my father related to us; he said: Jaʿfar b. ʿAwn related to us; he said: Abū ʿUmays related to us, from Qays b. Muslim, from Ṭāriq b. Shihāb, who said: ʿUmar said, “This verse — ‘al-yawma akmaltu lakum dīnakum’ — was revealed to the Messenger of God on the evening of ʿArafa, on a Friday.”

Its counterpart (naẓīrah) in the Madani-first and Shami counts is Sūrat Hūd; it has no counterpart in the others. Its words are two thousand eight hundred and four, and its letters are eleven thousand seven hundred and thirty-three.

It is one hundred and twenty verses in the Kufi count; one hundred and twenty-two in the two Madani counts, the Makki, and the Shami; and one hundred and twenty-three in the Basri.

Its points of disagreement are three verse-endings: “awfū bi-l-ʿuqūd” and “wa-yaʿfū ʿan kathīr” are not counted by the Kufi but are counted by the rest; “fa-innakum ghālibūn” is counted by the Basri and is not counted by the rest.

In it there are, by consensus, five places that resemble verse-endings without being counted: “ithnay ʿashara naqīban”; “qawman jabbārīn”; “sammāʿūna li-qawmin ākharīn”; “a-fa-ḥukma l-jāhiliyyati yabghūn”; and “min alladhīna stuḥiqqa ʿalayhimu l-awlayāni,” according to the reading of the one who reads it in the plural.

## رؤوس الآي note

The running رؤوس الآي are normalized here as a complete ordered sequence on the later-Madani baseline used elsewhere in the project. The local witness is defective at the top of the block: it preserves the unnumbered extra head “بالعقود”, then resumes only at “مبين / ١٥ مستقيم ...” and runs to a terminal blank “١٢٠”. In practice, that means the witness has lost the early common heads through “يصنعون”, omitted the extra disputed head “ويعفو عن كثير”, and dropped the expected display numeral before “مبين”. The reconstructed ordered list below restores ordinals 2–16 explicitly and keeps the surviving later segment as transmitted.
