## English translation

Sūrat Hūd.

Makkan. Its counterpart has already been mentioned in the Madani First and Shami counts, and it has no counterpart in the others. Its words are one thousand nine hundred and fifteen, and its letters are seven thousand five hundred and sixty-seven, the same as the letters of Yūnus. It is one hundred and twenty-one verses in the later-Madani, Makki, and Basri counts; one hundred and twenty-two in the Madani First and Shami counts; and one hundred and twenty-three in the Kufi count.

Its points of disagreement are seven verse-endings: “innī barīʾun mimmā tushrikūn” is counted by the Kufi and not counted by the others; “yujādilunā fī qawmi Lūṭ,” this being the second occurrence, is not counted by the Basri but counted by the others; all of them count “ilā qawmi Lūṭ,” which is the first occurrence; “min sijjīl” is counted by the later-Madani and the Makki and not counted by the others; “manḍūd” is not counted by the later-Madani and the Makki but counted by the others; “in kuntum muʾminīn” is counted by the two Madani counts and the Makki and not counted by the others; “wa-lā yazālūna mukhtalifīn” is not counted by the two Madani counts and the Makki but counted by the others; and “innā ʿāmilūn” is not counted by the later-Madani and the Makki but counted by the others.

In it there are, by consensus, six places that resemble verse-endings without being counted: “yaʿlamu mā yusirrūna wa-mā yuʿlinūna”; the first occurrence of “fa-sawfa taʿlamūn”; “wa-fāra l-tannūr”; “fīnā ḍaʿīfan”; the second occurrence of “sawfa taʿlamūn”; and “dhālika yawmun majmūʿun lahu l-nās.”
