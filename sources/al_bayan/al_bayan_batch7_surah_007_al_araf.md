## English translation

Sūrat al-Aʿrāf.

Makkan. Qatāda said: except for His saying, exalted is He, “wasʾalhum ʿani l-qaryah” — the verse — for it was revealed in Medina.

It has no counterpart (naẓīrah) in its verse-count.

Its words are three thousand three hundred and twenty-five, and its letters are fourteen thousand three hundred and ten. It is two hundred and five verses in the Basri and Shami counts, and two hundred and six in the two Madani counts, the Makki count, and the Kufi count.

Its points of disagreement are five verse-endings: “alif-lām-mīm-ṣād” is counted by the Kufi and not counted by the others; “mukhliṣīna lahu l-dīn” is counted by the Basri and the Shami and not counted by the others; “kamā badaʾakum taʿūdūn” is counted by the Kufi and not counted by the others; “ḍiʿfan mina l-nār” is counted by the two Madani counts and the Makki and not counted by the others; and the third occurrence of “al-ḥusnā ʿalā banī isrāʾīl” is likewise counted by the two Madani counts and the Makki and not counted by the others. All of them count the first and second occurrences of “banī isrāʾīl,” and they do not count the fourth occurrence of “banī isrāʾīl,” nor “mina l-jinni wa-l-insi fī l-nār.”

In it there are, by consensus, four places that resemble verse-endings without being counted: “fadallāhumā bi-ghurūr”; “Āla firʿawna bi-l-sinīn”; “wa-kharra mūsā ṣaʿiqan”; and “ʿadhāban shadīdan.”
