## English translation

Sūrat al-Tawbah.

Medinan. It has no counterpart (naẓīrah) in its verse-count.

Ibn ʿAbbās was asked about Sūrat al-Tawbah and said: “That is al-Fāḍiḥa. It kept coming down with ‘and among them … and among them …’ until we feared that it would leave no one unmentioned.”

Ḥudhayfa said: “You call this surah Sūrat al-Tawbah, but it is Sūrat al-ʿAdhāb. By God, it did not leave anyone without touching him.” The people of al-Madīna call it al-Tawbah, and the people of Makka call it al-Fāḍiḥa.

Its words are two thousand four hundred and ninety-seven, and its letters are ten thousand eight hundred and eighty-seven. It is one hundred and twenty-nine verses in the Kufi count and one hundred and thirty in the count of the others.

Its points of disagreement are three verse-endings: “anna llāha barīʾun mina l-mushrikīn” is counted by the Basri and not counted by the others; “illā tanfirū yuʿadhdhibkum ʿadhāban alīman,” the first occurrence, is counted by the Shami and not counted by the others; and “wa-ʿĀd wa-Thamūd” is counted by the two Madanis and the Makki and not counted by the others.

In it there are, by consensus, sixteen places that resemble verse-endings without being counted. The first is “illā lladhīna ʿāhadttum mina l-mushrikīn,” identified by the following phrase “thumma lam yanquṣūkum.” At that point the source pauses to note conflicting Basri reports concerning that location and concerning “barīʾun mina l-mushrikīn,” while stating that the sound Basri position is the one already given earlier and that the phrase at the opening of the surah is unanimously counted. The remaining places are: “wa-qātilū l-mushrikīn”; “bi-raḥmatin minhu wa-riḍwān”; “wa-qallabū laka l-umūr”; “wa-fī l-riqāb”; “wa-yuʾminu li-l-muʾminīn”; “man yalmizuka fī l-ṣadaqāt”; “yuʿadhdhibhumu llāhu ʿadhāban alīman,” the second occurrence; “mā ʿalā l-muḥsinīna min sabīl”; “wa-lā ʿalā lladhīna lā yajidūna mā yunfiqūn”; “mina l-muhājirīna wa-l-anṣār”; “wa-tafrīqan bayna l-muʾminīn”; “fa-yaqtulūna wa-yuqtalūn”; “an yastaghfirū li-l-mushrikīn”; “mā yattaqūn”; and “annahum yuftanūn.”
