## English translation

Sūrat al-Raʿd.

Makkan: this is the view of Ibn ʿAbbās, Mujāhid, Saʿīd b. Jubayr, and ʿAṭāʾ. Qatāda said that it is Medinan except for this verse, namely His saying, “wa-lā yazālu lladhīna kafarū tuṣībuhum bimā ṣanaʿū qāriʿah.”

Its counterpart (naẓīrah) in the two Madani counts and the Makki count is Sūrat Saʾala Sāʾil; in the Basri count, Sūrat Fāṭir, Qāf, and al-Nāziʿāt; and it has no counterpart in the Kufi or Shami count. Its words are eight hundred and fifty-five, and its letters are three thousand five hundred and six. It is forty-three verses in the Kufi count, forty-four in the two Madani counts and the Makki count, forty-five in the Basri count, and forty-seven in the Shami count.

Its points of disagreement are five verses: “lafī khalqin jadīd” is not counted by the Kufi but is counted by the rest; “qul hal yastawī l-aʿmā wa-l-baṣīr” is counted by the Shami and not counted by the rest; “am hal tastawī l-ẓulumātu wa-l-nūr” is not counted by the Kufi but is counted by the rest; “ulāʾika lahum sūʾ al-ḥisāb” is counted by the Shami and not counted by the rest; and “min kulli bāb” is not counted by the two Madani counts or the Makki count, but is counted by the rest.

In it there is, by consensus, one place that resembles a verse-ending without being counted: His saying, “wa-hum yakfurūna bi-l-raḥmān.”
