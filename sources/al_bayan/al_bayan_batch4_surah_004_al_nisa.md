## English translation

Sūrat al-Nisāʾ.

Medinan. It has no counterpart (naẓīrah) in its verse-count. Its words are three thousand nine hundred and forty-five, and its letters are sixteen thousand and thirty. It is one hundred and seventy-five verses in the two Madani counts, the Makki, and the Basri; one hundred and seventy-six in the Kufi; and one hundred and seventy-seven in the Shami.

Its points of disagreement are two verse-endings: “an taḍillū al-sabīl” is counted by the Kufi and the Shami, and the rest do not count it; “fa-yuʿadhdhibahum ʿadhāban alīman” is counted by the Shami, and the rest do not count it.

Abū al-Fatḥ, our shaykh, related to us; he said: Aḥmad b. Muḥammad related to us; he said: Aḥmad b. Shabīb related to us; he said: al-Faḍl related to us; he said: Khallād related to us, from ʿĪsā, from Ḥamza, from al-Aʿmash, from Ibrāhīm, from ʿAlqama, that he counted in Sūrat al-Nisāʾ “an taḍillū al-sabīl” as the head of verse forty-four.

In it there are six places that resemble verse-endings without being counted, by consensus: “falā tabghū ʿalayhinna sabīlan”; “lawlā akhkhartanā ilā ajalin qarīb”; “li-l-nāsi rasūlan”; “wa-Allāhu yaktubu mā yubayyitūn”; “wa-ittabiʿ millata Ibrāhīma ḥanīfan”; and “wa-lā al-malāʾikatu al-muqarrabūn.”
