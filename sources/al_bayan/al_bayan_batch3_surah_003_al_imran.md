## English translation

Sūrat Āl ʿImrān.

Medinan. It has no counterpart (naẓīrah) in its verse-count. Its words are three thousand four hundred and eighty, and its letters are fourteen thousand five hundred and twenty-five. It is two hundred verses in all systems of counting.

Its points of disagreement are seven verse-endings: “Alif Lām Mīm” is counted by the Kufi and not by the others; the first occurrence of “al-Injīl” is not counted by the Shami, while the others count it; “wa-anzala al-furqān” is not counted by the Kufi, while the others count it; the second occurrence of “al-Injīl” is counted by the Kufi and not by the others—and they all omit “al-Injīl” in al-Māʾida, al-Aʿrāf, and al-Fatḥ; “wa-rasūlan ilā banī isrāʾīl” is counted by the Basri and not by the others; “kāna ḥillan li-banī isrāʾīl” is not counted by any of them; and the first occurrence of “mimmā tuḥibbūn” is not counted by the Kufi, the Basri, or Abū Jaʿfar al-Qāriʾ, while the others and Shayba b. Niṣāḥ count it.

In it there are nine places that resemble verse-endings without being counted, by consensus: “lahum ʿadhābun shadīd”; “inna al-dīna ʿinda Allāhi al-islām”; “fī al-ummiyyīna sabīl”; “a-fa-ghayra dīni Allāhi yabghūn”; “ulāʾika lahum ʿadhābun alīm”; “man istaṭāʿa ilayhi sabīlan”; “min baʿdi mā arākum mā tuḥibbūn”; “yawma iltaqā al-jamʿān”; and “matāʿun qalīl.”
