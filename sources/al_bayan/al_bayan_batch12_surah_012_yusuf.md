## English translation

Sūrat Yūsuf.

Makkan. Its counterpart (naẓīrah) in the two Madani counts, the Makki count, and the Shami count is Sūrat al-Anbiyāʾ; in the Kufi count it is Sūrat Subḥān; and in the Basri count it is Sūrat al-Kahf and Sūrat al-Anbiyāʾ. Its words are one thousand and seventy-six, and its letters are seven thousand and forty-three. It is one hundred and eleven verses, with no disagreement in it.

In it there are, by consensus, four places that resemble verse-endings without being counted: “minhunna sikkīnan”, “maʿahu l-sijna fatayān”, “yaʾti baṣīran”, and “ʿibratan li-ulī l-albāb”.
